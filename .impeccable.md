## Design Context

### Users
FlowSelect is for desktop users who need a fast way to collect files, images, and web video into one controlled output folder. The product fits lightweight but frequent workflows, especially creators and operators who want a low-friction utility always available on the desktop edge.

### Brand Personality
Compact, intentional, polished.
The interface should feel utility-first, calm under pressure, and slightly neon without becoming flashy or playful.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the existing FlowSelect visual language: compact floating surfaces, layered dark/light gradients, minimal chrome, soft blue accent for active states, and small deliberate motion. Avoid loud warning blocks, generic flat gray UI, or decorative effects that overpower the tiny 200x200 main window.

### Design Principles
1. Match the established floating-window language before introducing any new styling pattern.
2. Reserve strong color for status emphasis and interactive focus, not for whole-panel decoration.
3. Keep compact surfaces dense but readable within two seconds.
4. Prefer semantic theme tokens and shared surface recipes over one-off component-local styling.
5. Use motion to clarify state, never to add ceremony.
